ORGANIC FERTILIZERS

Introduction to Organic Fertilizers

An organic fertilizer is a fertilizer that is derived from organic sources, including organic compost, cattle manures, poultry droppings and domestic sewage.The use of organic fertilizers has advantage of being cheap, improving soil structure.

COMPOST

Process of Compost

The composting process happens due to the activity of micro-organisms (bacteria) and other larger organisms like worms and insects. These need certain conditions to live. These include moisture and air.

Preparation of Compost

Compost can be prepared in many ways by using raw materials like farm waste, kitchen waste, town refuse, cow dung, cow urine. All these raw materials undergo aerobic or anaerobic decomposition in certain periods resulting in a finished product that is completely organic and rich in nutrients.

Factors of making a good compost

  • Type of organic material, air, moisture, and temperature.
  • The acidity (pH) is also considered by some to be an important factor.

    • How long does it take for compost to be ready ?

  • Compost can be made in as little as six to eight weeks, or, more usually, it can take a year or more.
  • In general, the more effort you put in, the quicker you will get compost.

    • How to identify that the compost is ready ?

    • The compost is ready for use when it's a rich brown colour.
    • The compost crumbles easily and also smells like earth.
    • Some signs that it may not be ready include: Recognizable food content still visible. The pile is still warm.

    How to manage ?

    Keep the compost pile moist, but not wet. The composting materials should feel damp like a wrung-out sponge. Turn the compost pile at least once or twice a month. Odors are emitted by poorly prepared or maintained compost piles.

    What are the benefits of Compost ?

  • Improves the structure and health of your soil by adding organic matter.
  • Helps the soil retain moisture and nutrients.
  • Reduces the potential for soil erosion.

    • MANURE

    Manure is a valuable soil amendment for home gardens. Animal manure is a valuable soil amendment for home gardens. It not only supplies primary nutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus and potassium) and micronutrients for plant growth, but also is a source of organic matter.

    Preparation of Manure

  • Manure is obtained from the decomposition of plant or animal wastes.
  • Plant and animal wastes are dumped in pits at open places and allow it to decompose by microbes.
  • The decomposition product is manure.

    • Types of Manure

    There are 2 types of manures :

  • Green Manure
  • Organic Manure

    • What is the best way to apply manure ?

    The best way to use manure as plant fertilizer is by mixing it in with compost. Composting manure eliminates the possibility of burning the plants.

    What are the factors of Manure ?

  • The primary factors that affect nutrient composition of manure are livestock type, stage of growth and feeding practices (all of which determine nutrient excretion rates).
  • he amount of bedding or water added to the manure, type of manure storage, time that the manure spends in storage and weather conditions.

    • How long does it take before it can be used as a manure?

  • Manure needs about 3–4 months to mature before it can be used as fertilizer, if conditions are ideal.
  • It can take a year or more if the starting material contains a wide carbon:nitrogen ratio (as is the case when manure contains wood chips).

    • How to manage ?

    The most known technology for manure management is the anaerobic digestion, which is a process in which organic matter from wet organic wastes (ie. liquid manure, food processing wastes, etc.) is converted into methane by bacteria in the absence of oxygen.

    BONE MEAL

    Bone Meal fertiliser is made by coarsely crushing animal bones and other waste from the slaughterhouse. It is an excellent source of phosphorus, nitrogen, and calcium in the plant. This fertiliser is made by coarsely crushing animal bones and other waste from the slaughterhouse.

    How to apply bone meal fertiliser ?

  • During soil preparation: Sprinkle bone meal fertiliser evenly over the soil or add to planting compost. Make sure it's mixed well. If the weather is dry, water in well.
  • In the growing season: If you're adding bone meal throughout the growing season, sprinkle evenly around established part of the soil and gently fork it into the surface. Be careful not to disturb plant roots as you do so. Water thoroughly.
  • Plants in containers: For established plants in containers, apply the bone meal fertiliser at the start of the season and then once or twice throughout the growing season to help strengthen plants. Again, sprinkle around the plants and work it into the soil being careful of any roots, then water well.
  • Vegetable plots: If you're using a bone meal for your vegetable plot, add fertiliser and fork it into the soil before planting any seeds.
  • New plants: Once you've made a planting hole fork a small amount of bone meal for plants into the hole. You can also add it to the compost and soil that will be used as the planting mixture. Water thoroughly.

    • What plants is bone meal best for?

    Bone meal fertilizer is an ideal soil additive for growing carrots, beets, potatoes, and other root crops. That's because of its high level of phosphorus, which aids in healthy root formation. Bone meal fertilizer can also help establish perennials during their first year.

    How long does it take for bone meal to break down in soil?

    Bone meal fertilizer takes about four months to fully break down in the soil, so don't reapply within this time period. Bone meal fertilizer makes a wonderful organic fertilizer to supply your plants with important nutrients like calcium and phosphorus, promoting strong, healthy plants and produce.

    What are the disadvantages of Bone Meal ?

  • Bone meal can be harmful when inhaled.
  • Bone meal is ineffective in alkaline soils.
  • Bone meal can hinder root development.

    • BLOOD MEAL

    Blood meal is a dry, inert powder made from blood, used as a high-nitrogen organic fertilizer and a high protein animal feed. N = 13.25%, P = 1.0%, K = 0.6%. It is one of the highest non-synthetic sources of nitrogen. It usually comes from cattle or hogs as a slaughterhouse by-product.

    How to use blood meal in the garden ?

  • New garden beds: Apply one to three pounds per 100 square feet.
  • New potted plants: Apply one to two teaspoons per gallon of soil.
  • Transplanting seedlings: Apply one teaspoon per hole.
  • Established garden plants: Apply one to two teaspoons around the root zone monthly.
  • Established potted plants: Apply one teaspoon per gallon of soil monthly during the growing season.

    • Which plants benefit from blood meal ?

    plants include asparagus, broccoli, corn, cucumbers, eggplant, okra, melons, peppers, pumpkins, squash, and tomatoes. It keeps plants fruiting, flowering and encourages lush green foliage. Use the intense smelling blood meal fertilizer to deter deer, moles, or ground squirrels.

    How Long Does Blood Meal Last In Soil ?

    Blood Meal is a slow-release fertilizer that can last around 2 to 6 weeks in soil.

    Why is blood meal called blood meal ?

    s the name implies, blood meal is a product that's derived directly from blood. Usually, this blood is a by-product of livestock production, coming from what would be waste material.

    What are the disadvantages of blood meal ?

  • Blood meal can burn your plants’ roots.
  • Blood meal can inhibit flower production.
  • Blood meal alters the pH of the soil.
  • Blood meal is not pet safe.

    • WROM CASTINGS

    Worm castings are an organic form of fertilizer produced from earthworms. Also known as vermicast, worm castings manure is essentially earthworm waste, otherwise known as worm poo. As these creatures eat through compost, their waste creates an optimal soil enricher.

    How many types of vermi are there ?

    Most worm farms raise two main types of earthworm:

  • Eisenia foetida
  • Lumbricus rubellis

    • Making of Worm Castings

    When making a worm castings bin, layer the bottom with sand and strips of moist newspaper. Then, add compost, manure, or leaf litter and another layer of moist newspaper strips and soil. Add some worms and food, such as kitchen scraps or garden waste.

    How long does it take to make worm castings ?

    Harvesting Castings from your Worm Bin

    After about three to six months of being fed, compost worms will have created enough compost to harvest. In this two bin system, when your bin is getting full you are ready to add the next layer of bin, nesting it on top.

    How do you control worm casts ?

    Earthworms are much less abundant in acidic soils, so in theory reducing the soil pH (acidification) should help to reduce a worm cast problem. Reducing food sources such as clippings and fallen leaves and top dressing with sand have also been suggested as methods of reducing earthworm numbers.

    What are the benefits of worm castings ?

  • Adds organic matter to the soil.
  • Improves soil Aeration.
  • Helps soil to absorb and hold water.
  • Reduces soil compaction.
  • Reduces soil erosion and crusting.
  • Improves soil structure.

    • GUANO

    Guano is the accumulated excrement of seabirds or bats. As a manure, guano is a highly effective fertilizer due to the high content of nitrogen, phosphate, and potassium, all key nutrients essential for plant growth.

    How Guano fertilizer is made ?

    Guano is derived from natural deposits of feces and urine from seabirds, bats, and seals collected from areas where favorable climatic conditions insured a minimal loss of nutrients through leaching. Either from seabirds in coastal areas with minimal rainfall.

    What are the types of guano fertilizers ?

    There are 2 types of guano fertilizers :

  • Seabird guano
  • Bat guano

    • How do you use guano fertilizer ?

    You can use guano as a top dressing fertilizer, either in wet or dried form. Simply mix it into the top layer of your soil prior to planting or during active growth.

    What are the Benefits of Guano Fertilizer?

  • Provides plants with complete nutrition.
  • Adds High organic matter to the soil for long-term improvement.
  • Improves soil texture.

    • What are the disadvantages of guano fertiliser ?

  • Guano is often more expensive than other types of manure.
  • It is difficult to find guano for sale in some areas.
  • Guano organic manure can be difficult to apply correctly.

    • FISH EMULSION

    Fish emulsion is an organic garden fertilizer that is made from whole fish or parts of fish.

    How do you make a fish emulsifier ?

    A fresh emulsion fertilizer mixture can be easily made from one-part fresh fish, three-parts sawdust, and one bottle of unsulfured molasses. It's usually necessary to add a little water too. Place the mixture in a large container with a lid, stirring and turning daily for about two weeks until the fish are broken down.

    What are the types fish emulsion fertilizers ?

    There are 3 types of fish emulsion fertilizers :

  • Fish meal
  • Hydrolyzed fish
  • Fish emulsion

    • How to use fish emulsion ?

    To use fish emulsion, you first need to dilute it. Add just 1/2 ounce (about two tablespoons) of fish emulsion to 1 gallon of water. The odor may be strong but will dissipate in a day or so. Then water the plants using that fish water.

    How fast does fish emulsion work ?

    Fish emulsion is fast-acting, meaning that your plants will absorb it more quickly than most fertilizers. For that reason, you should use it every two to three weeks.

    What are the minerals present in fish emulsion ?

  • Calcium
  • Magnesium
  • Chlorine
  • Sulphur
  • Sodium

    • What are the benefits of fish emulsion ?

  • Improves soil health.
  • Increases the root strength.

    • SEAWEED FERTILIZER

  • STEP: 3 - Spray the diluted seaweed liquid all over the plant or apply directly to the Soil.

Along with having the ability to improve resistance to disease, pests and abiotic plant stress.Too much spray of seaweed can burn seedlings and roots.
